# Brand Tone & Voice Guidelines

This instruction controls voice, tone, CTAs, and messaging consistency across all personalization ideas, experiment blueprints, and CMP plan content.

## Core Brand Personality

### Brand Archetype: **The Expert Guide**
Knowledgeable, trustworthy, and empowering - helping customers make informed decisions through personalized experiences that feel helpful, not intrusive.

### Brand Voice Attributes
- **Knowledgeable**: Demonstrate deep understanding without being condescending
- **Approachable**: Professional yet human, accessible to all skill levels
- **Confident**: Assertive in recommendations while respecting user choice
- **Empowering**: Help users achieve their goals through better experiences
- **Transparent**: Clear about personalization and data usage

## Tone Variations by Context

### Informational Content (Neutral-Helpful)
**Use for**: Educational content, feature explanations, how-to guides

**Characteristics**:
- Clear, direct language
- Focus on benefits and outcomes
- Use second person ("you")
- Include specific, actionable details
- Maintain professional but friendly tone

**Example Copy**:
✅ "Based on your recent activity, here are three solutions that match your specific needs..."
❌ "Our amazing products will revolutionize your workflow!"

### Promotional Content (Enthusiastic-Confident)
**Use for**: Product promotions, feature announcements, limited-time offers

**Characteristics**:
- Energetic but not pushy
- Emphasize value and urgency appropriately
- Use action-oriented language
- Include clear benefit statements
- Maintain authenticity

**Example Copy**:
✅ "Ready to streamline your process? This tool saves teams 5+ hours weekly."
❌ "Don't miss out! Limited time only! Buy now before it's too late!"

### Onboarding Content (Encouraging-Supportive)
**Use for**: Welcome sequences, getting started guides, first-time user experiences

**Characteristics**:
- Welcoming and reassuring
- Focus on progress and achievement
- Use inclusive language
- Celebrate milestones
- Provide clear next steps

**Example Copy**:
✅ "Great start! You're already set up for success. Here's what to tackle next..."
❌ "Congratulations! You've completed the basic setup process."

### Problem-Solving Content (Empathetic-Solution-Focused)
**Use for**: Error messages, troubleshooting, support content

**Characteristics**:
- Acknowledge user frustration
- Focus on solutions, not problems
- Use calming, reassuring language
- Provide multiple options when possible
- Include clear next steps

**Example Copy**:
✅ "We understand this is frustrating. Here are two ways to resolve this quickly..."
❌ "Error: This action cannot be completed. Please try again later."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Language Preferences

**Use Active Voice**:
✅ "We recommend this approach based on your goals"
❌ "This approach is recommended based on your goals"

**Write Conversationally**:
✅ "Here's what we found works best for teams like yours"
❌ "Our analysis indicates optimal solutions for similar organizational structures"

**Be Specific and Concrete**:
✅ "Save 2-3 hours per week with automated reporting"
❌ "Increase efficiency with our powerful automation features"

**Use Inclusive Language**:
✅ "For teams looking to improve collaboration"
❌ "For companies that want to get their employees to work better together"

### Sentence Structure
- Keep sentences under 25 words when possible
- Use parallel structure in lists
- Lead with benefits, follow with features
- Use transitional phrases to connect ideas
- Vary sentence length for natural rhythm

### Paragraph Guidelines
- One main idea per paragraph
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph in digital content
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Include white space for readability
- End paragraphs with clear next steps when appropriate

## Call-to-Action (CTA) Standards

### Primary CTAs (Conversion-Focused)

**High-Intent Actions**:
- "Start Your Free Trial"
- "Get Instant Access"
- "See How It Works"
- "Calculate Your Savings"
- "Schedule a Demo"

**Characteristics**:
- Action-oriented verbs (Start, Get, See, Try, Discover)
- Clear value proposition
- Create urgency without pressure
- 2-5 words when possible
- Specific to the user's journey stage

### Secondary CTAs (Engagement-Focused)

**Medium-Intent Actions**:
- "Learn More"
- "Read the Guide"
- "Watch the Demo"
- "Download the Report"
- "Join the Webinar"

**Characteristics**:
- Educational focus
- Lower commitment threshold
- Support primary conversion path
- Provide additional value
- Lead to primary CTAs

### Tertiary CTAs (Exploration-Focused)

**Low-Intent Actions**:
- "Browse Solutions"
- "Explore Features"
- "Read Customer Stories"
- "View Pricing"
- "Contact Sales"

**Characteristics**:
- No immediate commitment
- Support research phase
- Build trust and credibility
- Nurture future conversion
- Provide comprehensive information

## Personalization Messaging Framework

### Audience-Specific Messaging

**First-Time Visitors**:
- Focus on education and value demonstration
- Use welcoming, non-assumptive language
- Emphasize trust and credibility signals
- Provide clear navigation and next steps
- Avoid overly personalized messaging

**Return Visitors**:
- Reference previous interactions appropriately
- Focus on next logical steps in journey
- Use more direct, action-oriented language
- Highlight relevant new content or features
- Personalize based on demonstrated interests

**Engaged Prospects**:
- Use confident, solution-focused messaging
- Reference specific pain points and solutions
- Include social proof and case studies
- Create appropriate urgency
- Focus on conversion and commitment

**Existing Customers**:
- Use appreciative, relationship-focused tone
- Focus on optimization and expansion
- Highlight new features and capabilities
- Provide advanced tips and best practices
- Encourage community participation and advocacy

### Segment-Specific Adaptations

**SMB Customers**:
- Emphasize efficiency and cost-effectiveness
- Use practical, results-focused language
- Include quick-win opportunities
- Focus on simplicity and ease of use
- Highlight time-saving benefits

**Enterprise Customers**:
- Emphasize scale and comprehensive solutions
- Use strategic, outcome-focused language
- Include compliance and security messaging
- Focus on integration and customization
- Highlight ROI and business impact

**Technical Users**:
- Use precise, feature-focused language
- Include technical specifications when relevant
- Focus on capabilities and flexibility
- Provide detailed documentation links
- Highlight integration possibilities

**Business Users**:
- Use benefit-focused, outcome-oriented language
- Emphasize business impact and results
- Include success stories and case studies
- Focus on efficiency and productivity gains
- Highlight competitive advantages

## Emotional Tone Guidelines

### Positive Emotions to Evoke
- **Confidence**: "You're making the right choice"
- **Empowerment**: "Take control of your workflow"
- **Relief**: "Finally, a solution that just works"
- **Excitement**: "Discover what's possible"
- **Trust**: "Join thousands of successful teams"

### Emotions to Avoid
- **Anxiety**: Avoid fear-based messaging
- **Confusion**: Don't overcomplicate explanations
- **Pressure**: Avoid aggressive sales tactics
- **Frustration**: Don't highlight problems without solutions
- **Overwhelm**: Keep choices manageable and clear

## Content Formatting Standards

### Headlines and Subheads
- Use sentence case (capitalize only first word and proper nouns)
- Keep headlines under 60 characters for web
- Use benefit-focused language
- Include keywords naturally
- Create clear hierarchy with subheads

### Lists and Bullets
- Use parallel structure throughout
- Lead with action verbs when possible
- Keep items concise and scannable
- Use consistent punctuation
- Limit to 7±2 items when possible

### Links and CTAs
- Use descriptive link text (avoid "click here")
- Match link text to destination page
- Use consistent styling across touchpoints
- Test different variations regularly
- Ensure accessibility compliance

## Cross-Channel Consistency

### Email Personalization
- Use subscriber's name appropriately (not excessively)
- Reference previous interactions naturally
- Maintain consistent brand voice
- Adapt content length for channel
- Include clear unsubscribe options

### Website Personalization
- Maintain consistent navigation and layout
- Adapt messaging without changing core UX
- Use progressive disclosure for complex information
- Ensure fast loading times
- Test across devices and browsers

### Mobile Optimization
- Use shorter headlines and copy blocks
- Prioritize essential information
- Use larger, thumb-friendly CTAs
- Minimize form fields and inputs
- Test on various screen sizes

## Quality Assurance Checklist

### Before Publishing Any Personalized Content:
- [ ] Voice and tone match brand guidelines
- [ ] Language is appropriate for target audience
- [ ] CTAs are clear and action-oriented
- [ ] Messaging supports user journey stage
- [ ] Content is inclusive and accessible
- [ ] Links and functionality work properly
- [ ] Mobile experience is optimized
- [ ] Personalization enhances (doesn't distract from) core message
- [ ] Privacy and data usage is handled appropriately
- [ ] Content aligns with overall campaign objectives

### Testing and Optimization:
- [ ] A/B test different tone variations
- [ ] Monitor engagement metrics by audience segment
- [ ] Collect qualitative feedback when possible
- [ ] Regular brand compliance audits
- [ ] Update guidelines based on performance data
